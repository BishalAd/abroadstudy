Can I Do It Myself?

Yes — You Can Self-Apply

Every country on this list allows you to self-apply without an agent. Here's how for each. Save your NPR 50,000–1,50,000 in agent fees.

Germany — Step by step
1
Research programs on daad.de or hochschulstart.de
2
Apply via uni-assist.de (most public unis) or direct to private university website
3
Open blocked account (Fintiba or Expatrio — fully online)
4
Book German Embassy Kathmandu visa appointment online
5
After arrival: register at Einwohnermeldeamt (city registration office)
6
Get residence permit at local Ausländerbehörde
🇳🇱
Netherlands — Step by step
1
Browse programs at studyfinder.nl or university websites
2
Apply via Studielink.nl — the national application portal
3
University forwards your application to IND for MVV visa
4
Collect MVV visa at Dutch Embassy Kathmandu or Delhi
5
After arrival: IND appoints you for biometrics & issues residence permit
6
Register at municipality (gemeente) for BSN number
🇵🇱

Browse programs at irk.irk.pl or directly at university websites
2
Apply directly to university online, receive acceptance letter
3
Apply for Polish National Visa (D) at Polish Embassy, New Delhi (no embassy in Nepal)
4

5
Register at local university as enrolled student

5
Apply for student visa at Hungarian Embassy (via Delhi or online)
6
After arrival: register at NDGAP (enterhungary.gov.hu) for residence permit

Important Advice

Pro Tips for Nepali Students

Hard-earned advice that most consultants won't tell you.

🎯
Your Best Country Ranking (for your profile)
Based on IELTS 6.0 + GPA 2.54 + goal of study+earn+PR: 1. Germany (if you learn German), 2. Norway (if you have funds), 3. Poland (easiest entry), 4. Hungary (try for scholarship), 5. Czech Republic, 6. Finland (target AMK), 7. Netherlands (target HBO), 8. Italy, 9. Greece.
💰
Never Rely on Part-Time Work for Living Costs
Part-time job income is unpredictable — it takes months to find work after arrival. Always have 6 months of living expenses saved before you go. Budget at least NPR 8–15 lakhs as emergency fund on top of the visa requirement money.
📝
Write a Powerful Statement of Purpose (SOP)
With GPA 2.54, your SOP is your most important tool. Explain your career goals clearly, why you chose this specific country and program, what you've learned from your work experience (Biznity / IT Manager role), and your concrete plan. A great SOP can overcome a lower GPA.
🗣
Start Learning the Local Language NOW
Every country requires some language for PR. German or Norwegian will triple your employment options. Even Polish or Czech basics help enormously. Use free resources: Duolingo (Polish, Czech, Norwegian), Goethe Institut Kathmandu (German), YouTube channels. Start 12 months before applying.
⚠️
NEVER Break Your Residence Permit
Any gap in your legal residence resets your PR clock. Never let your permit expire — renew 2–3 months before the expiry date. Never stay illegally even for one day. Never travel and come back with an expired permit. The PR countdown only works with continuous legal residence.
🏦
Blocked Account / Proof of Funds — Plan Early
Germany needs NPR 17–18 lakhs blocked, Norway needs ~NPR 15 lakhs, Netherlands needs bank proof. These amounts must sit in your account before visa. Start saving NOW, 1–2 years before you plan to leave. Look into whether a family sponsor (Verpflichtungserklärung in Germany) is an option.
